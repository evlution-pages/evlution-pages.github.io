Norway is heading to 100 percent adoption of Electric Vehicles

By @Ellec on Sat, 22nd of June, 2019 at 03:06PM ET

Norway is the undeniable leader when it comes to adoption of Battery Electric Vehicles (BEV) with more then 60% of new cars being electric. However that share could be less than actual demand as dealers and car companies can't meet the demands of the Norwegian consumers.

The Scandinavian country is looking to ban fossil fuelled vehicles by 2025 which is one of the most aggressive targets at the national level. This has undoubtedly been a forcing function in the swift adoption with traditional petrol and diesel cars plummeting in value. Another driver in this rapid growth is the government incentives that go back as far as 1990, zero sales tax and perks such as driving in bus lanes are further incentives.

It's clear from the Norway situation that incentives and bans drive change, and with many other countries bans following 5-10 years later it looks like traditional fossil fuel vehicles days are numbered.
