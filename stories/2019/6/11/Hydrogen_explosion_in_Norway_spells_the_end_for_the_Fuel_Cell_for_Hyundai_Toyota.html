Hydrogen explosion in Norway spells the end for the Fuel Cell for Hyundai Toyota

By Ellec on Tuesday the 11th of June, 2019 at 01:06PM @ellec_uk

News of an explosion in the Uno-X refueling hydrogen station in Sandvika in Bærum has delivered a death blow to the nascent Fuel Cell vehicle technology.

Both Toyota and Hyundai have chosen to bet on this new style of vehiclerather than the Battery Electric Vehicle most other auto makers seem to be backing.

There is still no clue on what caused the explosion and CEO of the company operating the station said

“It is too early to speculate on the cause and what has gone wrong. Our top priority is the safe operation of the stations we have delivered. As a precaution, we have temporarily put ten other stations in standby mode in anticipation of more information.”
Although Toyota are publicly still behind the technology they have stopped sales until the situation is clearer.
